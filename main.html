Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. It's famous for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It's also known for its formal traditions such as kaiseki dining and geisha female entertainers.

  1. Fushimi Inari Taisha: The famous shrine known for its thousands of vermilion torii gates.
  2. Kinkaku-ji: The Golden Pavilion, a stunning Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: A beautiful bamboo forest that is a popular spot for tourists.
  4. Nijo Castle: A historic castle that was the residence of the Tokugawa shoguns.
  5. Gion District: The famous geisha district with traditional wooden machiya houses.
  • Kaiseki: A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner.
  • Yudofu: A hot tofu dish that is especially popular in Kyoto.
  • Matcha Tea: Traditional powdered green tea used in Japanese tea ceremonies.
  • Shojin Ryori: A type of vegetarian cuisine associated with Buddhist monks.
  • Kyoto Sweets: Traditional Japanese sweets such as Yatsuhashi.
